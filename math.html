A math thing.

More precisely, a problem concerning Stanley-Reisner rings and their Hilbert series.

TLDR:

The automorphism group of an abstract simplicial complex \(\Delta\) acts on the polynomial ring on the vertexes of \(\Delta\), and thus on the numerator of the Hilbert series of the Stanley-Reisner ring of \(\Delta\). Understand this action in terms of the combinatorics and homology of \(\Delta\). Because it is a bit clunky to write math in HTML, you can see this abomination of a bacholor thesis for more details (especially section 5 and onward).

Introduction

Let \(n > 0\) be an integer, and \(\Delta\) an abstract simplicial complex on a vertex set indexed by \([n] = \{1, ..., n\}\). Let \(k\) be a field (of characteristic zero, say) and let \(M = R_\Delta\) be the finitely generated monomial \(k\)-algebra generated by the vertices of \(\Delta\), subject to a set of monomial relations which we will define. That is, \(M = S/I\), where \(S = k[x_1, ..., x_n]\), and \(I = I_\Delta\) is to be defined. We remark that we might as well say that the vertices of \(\Delta\) are exactly \(x_1, ..., x_n\), and we will.

Definitions

Let \(\{x_{i_1}, ...,x_{i_r}\}\) be a simplex in \(\Delta\). We identify it with the product \(x_{i_1}\cdot ... \cdot x_{i_r} \in k[x_1, ..., x_n]\). Then let \(I_\Delta\) be the ideal of \(S\) generated by all products \(\prod_{i \in J} x_i\), ranging over all non-simplexes \(J\) of \(\Delta\). This is a squarefree monomial ideal. The quotient \(S/I_\Delta\) is the Stanley-Reisner ring associated to the abstract simplicial complex \(\Delta\).

Example

Let \(\Delta\) be the Scarf complex naturally on \(x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4\). Then \(I_\Delta = (x_1x_4, x_2x_4)\), since the only simplexes (in this case edges) missing are the ones connecting the triangle to the lone edge sticking out of the triangle.


The Stanley-Reisner ring \(R_\Delta\) is naturally a graded module over the graded ring \(S\), both considered with the standard \(\mathbf{N}^n\)-grading, and as such it has a Hilbert-Poincaré series.

Theorem

Let \(\Delta\) be an abstract simplicial complex as above, and \(R\) its Stanley-Reisner ring. The Hilbert-Poincaré series \(HS_M(x_1, ..., x_n; \Delta)\) of \(R\) is equal to $$ \sum_{\sigma \in \Delta} \prod_{i \in \sigma} \frac{x_i}{1-x_i} = \frac{\sum_{\sigma \in \Delta}\prod_{i \in \sigma} x_i \prod_{i \notin \sigma} 1-x_i}{\prod_{i = 1}^n 1-x_i}$$ The proof is not difficult, and is not given here.


The Hilbert-Poincaré series, and especially its numerator, summarises much of the homological information of \(R_\Delta\). Anything we say from here on is equivalently stated in terms of free resolutions or Betti numbers.

Some combinatorics

To \(\Delta\) we associate its automorphism group \(G = \operatorname{aut}(\Delta)\). Then \(G\) acts naturally on \(S\) and \(R\). Let \(K\) be a vertex set of \(\Delta\), and denote by \(\operatorname{orb}(K)\) its orbit under the action of \(G\). Let \(x^K = \prod_{i \in K} x_i\) be the mononmial associated to \(K\). Define $$s_K = \sum_{L \in \operatorname{orb}(K)}x^L$$ as the (elementary) graph-symmetric polynomial associated to \(K\). Clearly \(s_K = s_L\) whenever \(K,L\) lie in the same orbit.

Example (to explain the name)

Let \(\Delta\) be a graph on a \([n]\) (in the ordinary sense), and note that it is also an abstract simplicial complex by viewing the edges as the simplices. If \(\Delta = K_n\), the complete graph on \(n\) vertices, then \(\operatorname{aut}(\Delta) = S_n\), the symmetric group on \(n\) letters, and the elementary graph-symmetric polynomials are exactly the elementary symmetric polynomials.
