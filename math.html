A math thing.

More precisely, a problem concerning Stanley-Reisner rings and their Hilbert series.

Introduction

Let \(n > 0\) be an integer, and \(\Delta\) an abstract simplicial complex on a vertex set indexed by \([n] = \{1, ..., n\}\). Let \(k\) be a field (of characteristic zero, say) and let \(M = R_\Delta\) be the finitely generated monomial \(k\)-algebra generated by the vertices of \(\Delta\), subject to a set of monomial relations which we will define. That is, \(M = S/I\), where \(S = k[x_1, ..., x_n]\), and \(I = I_\Delta\) is to be defined. We might as well say that the vertices of \(\Delta\) are exactly \(x_1, ..., x_n\).

Now, let \(\{x_{i_1}, ...,x_{i_r}\}\) be a simplex in \(\Delta\). We identify it with the product \(x_{i_1}\cdot ... \cdot x_{i_r} \in k[x_1, ..., x_n]\). Then let \(I_\Delta\) be the ideal of \(S\) generated by all products \(\prod_{i \in J} x_i\), ranging over all non-simplexes \(J\) of \(\Delta\).

Example

Let \(\Delta\) be the Scarf complex naturally on \(x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4\). Then \(I_\Delta = (x_1x_4, x_2x_4)\), since the only simplexes (in this case edges) missing are the ones connecting the triangle to the lone edge sticking out of the triangle.
